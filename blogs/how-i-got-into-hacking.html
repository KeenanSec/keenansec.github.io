HOW I GOT INTO HACKING

It started in first grade. While other kids were drawing or playing outside, I was glued to the family computer — not playing games, but clicking through every menu, every setting, every option I could find. I didn't know what I was doing. I just knew I needed to understand how it worked.

// THE TURNING POINT

By the time I was 15, I'd stumbled across a YouTube video about SQL injection. I remember the exact feeling — this idea that you could talk to a database through a web form. That a few characters could bypass an entire login system. I was hooked.

I spent the next few weeks going down every rabbit hole I could find. Kali Linux. Metasploit. Wireshark. None of it made complete sense at first, but I didn't care. I was learning the language of machines.

// FIRST CTF

My first real CTF was a disaster. I solved one challenge in five hours. One. But I didn't feel defeated — I felt like I finally had a benchmark. A place to measure what I didn't know yet. 

$ nmap -sV -sC target.htb
# I had no idea what half these flags did
# but I was running them anyway

That's the thing about this field. You don't need to know everything. You just need to be comfortable not knowing, and keep moving forward anyway.

// WHERE I AM NOW

I'm still learning. Every CTF, every writeup, every rabbit hole teaches me something new. This blog is where I document that journey — the wins, the frustrations, and everything in between.

If you're just starting out: stay curious. Break things. Read writeups even when you don't understand them. You will eventually.
